Rules of Yahtzee

The aim of the game is to roll 5 dice and create certain 'Hands' by analogy to poker. You can roll upto three times and then you must pick a hand to use your dice on. Between rolls you can 'lock' some dice and reroll others.

You can only select each hand once. After selecting a hand it's the next player's turn. After all players are unable to select any more hands, the game is over and the scores are added up to see who won.

You must roll at least once before you choose a hand. Click the dice to lock or unlock them and click the name of the hand you want to use your dice to score on.

The 'Upper Section' is the simplest, you just count how many dice you have of a give value, and add them up to get the score

'Sum' is adding up all of the upper section scores. If you have a sum of 63 or greater, you get a bonus 35 points

The 'Lower Section' has hands similar to poker. 3 of a kind and 4 of a kind are scored by adding up all of your dice if you meet the requirement, but score zero otherwise

Full House means you have 3 of one dice and 2 of another. It gives 25 points.

Small straight means having four sequential dice. It scores 30 points.

Large straight means having all five sequential dice. It scores 40 points

Yahtzee is having five of a kind. It scores 50 points. If you've already used up your yahtzee slot, it also counts as full house, 4 of a kind and 3 of a kind.

Chance is simply the all of your dice added together, with no special requirements. It's good to use if you have high values but don't meet anything else.
